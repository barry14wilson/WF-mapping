Introduction

Why London — and what to know first

One of the world's great capitals, London rewards every kind of traveller. Two thousand years of history sit alongside world-class museums, royal palaces, riverside walks and a food scene drawn from every corner of the planet — and most of the best stuff is free.

Straddling the River Thames, London is a city of distinct villages — each borough with its own character, pace and personality. Whether you're after the polish of Kensington, the buzz of Shoreditch, or the leafy calm of Richmond, there's a corner of London for every kind of trip.

According to the UN Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), England and Wales recorded 123.3 violent offences per 100,000 population in 2023 — a 45% decline from the 2008 peak. Within London, crime is highly borough-specific: outer residential boroughs consistently record the lowest crime rates, while central tourist hotspots show concentrated street crime.

Camden is one of London's most-visited areas — Camden Market, Regent's Canal, the music venues, the food halls. It's also one of the higher-crime boroughs in central London, with street crime and drug activity most concentrated around Camden High Street, Camden Lock and Kentish Town late at night.

The honest take: Camden is absolutely worth visiting. During the day and early evening the market and canal are lively and safe — keep your bag closed, phone out of sight, and treat it like any busy tourist hub. After 10pm, stick to well-lit main routes (Camden High Street, Parkway) and take a licensed cab back rather than walking long stretches alone.

Where to base yourself if Camden isn't for you: Kensington & Chelsea is the safest central borough for visitors. South Bank, Victoria and Bloomsbury are also good central, low-friction bases.

Practical safety

Five tips that will save you a bad day

1
Stay aware on the Tube and in crowded tourist areas — pickpocketing is the most common issue for visitors.
2
Keep bags zipped and phones out of sight on busy streets, particularly around Oxford Street, Camden and Leicester Square.
3
London's black cabs (licensed taxis) and Uber are both safe and regulated. Avoid unlicensed minicabs.
4
Emergency services: dial 999 (police, fire, ambulance). Non-emergency police: 101.
5
The NHS provides emergency healthcare free of charge to all visitors at A&E departments.
